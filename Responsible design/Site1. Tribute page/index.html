Jonas Salk

"Miracle worker"

Magazine photo of Jonas Salk (O'Neill, William L. (1989)

Here's a timeline of Jonas Salk's life:

  • 1914 - Born in New York City, USA.

  • 1927 - Salk entered Townsend Harris High School.

  • 1934 - He earned a Bachelor of Science degree in chemistry in CCNY.

  • 1939 - He earned his medical degree from the New York University School of Medicine and became a scientist physician at Mount Sinai Hospital. The day after his graduation from medical school in 1939, Salk married Donna Lindsay, a master's candidate at the New York College of Social Work.

  • 1942 - Salk went to the University of Michigan on a research fellowship to develop an influenza vaccine.

  • 1947 - Salk was appointed director of the Virus Research Laboratory at the University of Pittsburgh School of Medicine. With funding from the National Foundation for Infantile Paralysis — now known as the March of Dimes Birth Defects Foundation —he began to develop the techniques that would lead to a vaccine to wipe out the most frightening scourge of the time: paralytic poliomyelitis.

  • 1954 - National testing of Salk's vaccine began on one million children, ages six to nine, who became known as the Polio Pioneers.

  • 1955 - The results were announced: the vaccine was safe and effective. Hailed as a miracle worker, Salk never patented the vaccine or earned any money from his discovery, preferring it be distributed as widely as possible. Salk's inactivated polio vaccine is on the World Health Organization's List of Essential Medicines, the safest and most effective medicines needed in a health system.

  • 1963 - Founding the Salk Institute for Biological Studies in La Jolla. He was aided with a $20 million grant from the National Science Foundation and support from the March of Dimes.

  • 1980s - Salk engaged in research to develop a vaccine for AIDS. He cofounded The Immune Response Corporation (IRC) with Kevin Kimberlin and patented Remune, an immunologic therapy, but was unable to secure liability insurance for the product. The project was discontinued in 2007, twelve years after Salk's death.

  • 1995 - He died at the age of 80 in La Jolla, California.
